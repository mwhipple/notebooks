#+title: browser-sledgehammer

The browser sledgehammer is a term I just made up so I could point to
it.

Web browsers provide a convenient, low overhead way to deliver
functionality to users (ignoring for a moment some underlying concerns
related to moats and isolation) and are therefore often a core part of
the engine that drives some tools.

Unfortunately browsers are also complicated beasts that are primarily
oriented for interactive use, so this can also introduce a massive
dependency for functionality that could otherwise remain relatively
simple. How this manifests for the Electron framework is touched on
here in terms of bloat and supply chain risks:
https://spectrum.ieee.org/lean-software-development

Often this translates to the automation of such functionality
requiring passing things through browser automation tooling, which is
in the best case very heavy-handed, but can also introduce additional
challenges if the automation would otherwise be running in an
environment where such browsers and their pre-requisites would
otherwise not be available (like many containerized build
environments). This effectively translates to a range of
costs due to some logic incidentally relying on browser provided
functionality, which often translates to simple inputs that produce
simple outputs but drag in that massive additional system to do so.
