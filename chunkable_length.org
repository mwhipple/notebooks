#+title: chunkable-length

I try to keep all of my construct within a reasonable length. While
this often gets reduced to hard rules, I adopt an approach that adapts
based on the cotnext. There's also often a tension between how large
things are and how many things there are, and also between splitting
things up just to potentially need to stitch them back together.

My preferred length is not set in stone and varies based on what is
afforded by the language and systems that I'm using. Languages that
provide strong namespacing/modularity and composability that allows
for dense code may lend itself to consistently bite-sized pieces,
whereas systems that lack one or more of those may warrant larger
pieces. What the language provides will also inform how I construct
the system, for those that provide ample means of combination and
modularit I'm likely to prefer defining pieces and combining them,
whereas for those that do not I'm more likely to split things out as
they reach a certain threshold.

All of the above is ultimately driven by cognitive overhead and the
magic number seven (plus or minus two). If any given piece seems like
it has too much information for me to squeeze into my pre-frontal
cortex I'll look at splitting it up into chunks that can. This can
certainly be subjective, but I generally try to follow the idea that
if if something can't be easily digested once it will be again, and
should be broken down to try to help my future self or others.
