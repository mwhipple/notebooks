#+title: serde

serde is common shorthand for serialization/deserialization in the
spirit of similar terms like modem and codec. I personally like
marshun which I've used in the past...but I don't know of anyone else
that actually used marshun whereas serde seems to pop up a bit.

While it may often have more specific connotations in certain
contexts, it can more generally refer to the translation logic that
connects a model to a wire representation and can be extended to be an
enabler for goals like hexagonal architecture and MVP style separation
of concerns. Specifically serde seems to be primarily used from the
technical perspective ("here's how to get your data to work with our
infrastructure") whereas I'd also use it from the other perpsective
("here's how to insulate business logic from infrastructure").
