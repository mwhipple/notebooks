#+title: ai-models

Selection of AI models should be driven by quantifiable metrics.

With local inference particularly during devleopment, one of the
biggest considerations is performance, and therefore I'll default to a
model that I have locally that seems to provide the lowest latency,
and will iterate from there.

Iteration will involve both different models, different variants of
the models, and possible optimizations applied to the models. This
should all be driven by having a decent harness.

** gpt-oss
:PROPERTIES:
:ID:       D6EB63CC-2B15-45DC-8C49-33C0DB4DA1C5
:END:

~gpt-oss~ is my default as I coincidentally downloaded it initially in
support of some project or other and it seems to have decent
performance. It seems likely to get displaced as the default over
time.

** qwen3
:PROPERTIES:
:ID:       265230C1-96C3-4C5B-8CE3-050F471FCFF2
:END:

~qwen3~ is anecdotally more optimized for most of the purposes I'd be
looking for. In practice it does seem to be far better at producing
the desired results but the default overhead is /much/ higher. At the
moment I'm therefore likely to do rough passess with ~gpt-oss~ and
then bring in ~qwen3~ after things are somewhat built.
