#+title: list

Lists are one of the most fundamental compound data types in
programming languages.

This treatment will draw primarily from the Abstract Data Type concept
of Aho, Hopcroft, and Ullman.

These represent a sequence of elements, with guarantees around
ordering, and without inherent uniqueness guarantees.

The element types may be heterogeneous, or may be constrained
depending on things like language (and approaches such as unions and
sum types provide typed heterogeneity anyway).

The underlying storage can be virtually anything, though variations of
contiguous arrays and [[id:3B936526-69D4-4C9D-AE47-D76AA3997388][linked-lists]] are particularly common.
